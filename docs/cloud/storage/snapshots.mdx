# Tenbyte Cloud Snapshots
This guide shows you how to create and manage snapshots for your virtual machines in **Tenbyte Cloud**.


## 1. Select Compute Section
- From the left sidebar, click on **Compute** to view your created virtual machines (VMs).

    <Frame caption="View your created Virtual Machines">
        <img src="/images/cloud/25.png" alt="View your created Virtual Machines" />
    </Frame>

## 2. Choose Your Virtual Machine
- In the Compute section, you will see a list of all your created VMs.

    <Frame caption="Select the VM">
        <img src="/images/cloud/27.png" alt="Select the VM" />
    </Frame>

- Select the VM you want to manage. 

## 3. Navigate to the Snapshots Tab
- After selecting your VM, you will see a detailed overview of the machine.
- Click on the **Snapshots** tab to manage snapshot for the selected VM.

    <Frame caption="Navigate to the Snapshot Tab">
        <img src="/images/cloud/33.png" alt="Navigate to the Snapshot Tab" />
    </Frame>

## 4. Take a Snapshot
- To create a snapshot, click the **Take snapshot** button.
    <Frame caption="Take a Snapshot">
        <img src="/images/cloud/34.png" alt="Take a Snapshot" />
    </Frame>


## 5. Snapshot Details
- After taking a snapshot, the details will be listed here:

    1. **UUID**: The unique identifier for each snapshot.
    2. **Size**: The size of the snapshot.
    3. **Creation** Date: The date and time the snapshot was created.

    <Frame caption="Snapshot Details">
        <img src="/images/cloud/35.png" alt="Snapshot Details" />
    </Frame>

## 6. Manage Snapshots

    - **Restore**: Use the snapshot to restore your VM to the captured state.
    - **Create New VM**: You can also create a new VM based on the snapshot with modified resources.

        <Frame caption="Create a New VM from Snapshot">
            <img src="/images/cloud/36.png" alt="Create a New VM from Snapshot" />
        </Frame>
    <Note>
        After creating a VM from a snapshot, you can reconfigure the VM by modifying its resources and settings.
    </Note>

    - **Delete**: If no longer needed, you can delete the snapshot by clicking the trash icon.
