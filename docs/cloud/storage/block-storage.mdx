# Tenbyte Cloud Block Storage

This guide shows you how to **create** and **manage block storage** for your virtual machines in **Tenbyte Cloud**.


## 1. Select Compute Section
- From the left sidebar, click on **Compute** to view your created virtual machines (VMs).

    <Frame caption="View your created Virtual Machines">
        <img src="/images/cloud/25.png" alt="View your created Virtual Machines" />
    </Frame>

## 2. Choose Your Virtual Machine
- In the Compute section, you will see a list of all your created VMs.

    <Frame caption="Select the VM">
        <img src="/images/cloud/27.png" alt="Select the VM" />
    </Frame>

- Select the VM you want to manage. 

## 3. Navigate to the Disks Tab
- After selecting your VM, you will see a detailed overview of the machine.
- Click on the **Disks** tab to manage storage settings for the selected VM.

    <Frame caption="Navigate to the Disks Tab">
        <img src="/images/cloud/37.png" alt="Navigate to the Disks Tab" />
    </Frame>

## 4. View Existing Disks
- The Disks section will display the disks attached to your VM. 

    <Frame caption="View Existing Disks">
        <img src="/images/cloud/39.png" alt="View Existing Disks" />
    </Frame>

- You may see the Boot Disk with details such as:

    - **UUID**: The unique identifier for the disk.
    - **Size**: The disk size.
    - **Created**: The creation timestamp.
    - **Est. Monthly Cost**: The cost for the disk storage.

## 5. Add Additional Disk
- To add a new disk, click on **Add additional disk**. You can choose the disk size based on your requirements and attach it to the VM.
- Click on the **Add** to add additional disk.

    <Frame caption="Add Additional Disk">
        <img src="/images/cloud/38.png" alt="Add Additional Disk" />
    </Frame>

## 6. Manage Disks
- You can **resize**, or **delete existing disks**. 
- Next to the disk you want to resize, click on the **Resize** button.
- Adjust the disk size according to your needs by modifying the disk size.
- After setting the desired size, click **Resize** to apply the changes.

    <Frame caption="Resize your Disk">
        <img src="/images/cloud/41.png" alt="Resize your Disk" />
    </Frame>

<Warning>
    It is highly recommended to stop the virtual machine before resizing the boot disk to prevent any issues with the operating system.
</Warning>
